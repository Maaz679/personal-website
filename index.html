About Me

I study Electrical Engineering at San Jose State University, focused on power systems. Getting solar, storage, and flexible loads to work inside a grid that was never designed for them takes engineers who understand both the physics and the software. That is where I want to work.

I learn by building things. Right now that looks like pulling real market data from CAISO, writing economic dispatch models based on textbook theory, and simulating EMS control logic against actual physical constraints.

Outside of engineering, I teach Quran at Masjid Muhajireen to kids and adults. Explaining hard material to people at different levels has made me a better engineer. I also volunteer with KiloWatts for Humanity, a nonprofit that installs solar microgrids in off-grid communities.
